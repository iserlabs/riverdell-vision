## 0. The original site vs. v2 (an honest assessment)

The previous site was a competent template on Roya's platform. It got the basics
right, but it presented a top-tier, specialty-driven practice as an ordinary local
optometrist, and left real value on the table.

**What the old site did well (kept and strengthened in v2):**
- Showed your genuine 5.0 Google rating and the insurers you accept.
- Offered real online booking through Zocdoc.
- Listed your address, hours, and credentialed doctors.
- Had a page title aimed at "Optometrist in Oradell."

**Where it fell short (fixed in v2):**
- It buried your most valuable, most defensible services (myopia management, dry eye,
  specialty and scleral lenses, vision therapy, neuro-rehabilitation) inside dropdown
  menus, so the practice read like a general glasses shop instead of the specialty
  practice it is.
- The homepage led with "Welcome to Riverdell Vision," which says nothing, and the
  headline was being cut off on screen by the booking widget sitting on top of it.
- It looked like a template: a generic blue, stock fonts, and a flat, monotone design
  that did not reflect a premium, physician-led practice.
- Almost none of its images had descriptive text ("alt text"), which hurts both
  accessibility and Google.
- It leaned on a bolt-on "accessibility widget," a band-aid that can itself create legal
  exposure for a medical practice, instead of real, built-in accessibility.

**Old vs. new, in a sentence:** the old site undersold you. v2 surfaces the specialty
care that is your actual value, presents it like the premium practice it is, and adds the
search, reputation, and conversion engine underneath (Sections 2 and 3).

## 3. What you CANNOT see, but is working 24/7 (the engine)

This is where most of the value lives. None of it is visible to a patient, all of it
drives views, bookings, reputation, and ranking.

**To get found on Google (SEO):**
- Every page has its own optimized title, description, and canonical link, a clean
  single headline structure, and internal links, targeted at the exact phrases patients
  search ("optometrist Oradell NJ," "myopia management Bergen County," "scleral lenses,"
  and so on).
- A full sitemap and crawl instructions tell Google exactly what to index.
- Dedicated, keyword-specific pages for each service and each nearby town, the same
  "programmatic local SEO" a premium agency builds by hand.

**To get cited by AI search (AEO, the next frontier most agencies are not doing yet):**
- An `llms.txt` file and explicit permissions that invite ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google's
  AI, and Claude to read and cite the practice.
- Plain-English "short answer" blocks and FAQ sections on every service page, written so
  an AI assistant can quote them when someone asks "who does myopia management near
  Oradell?"

**To power Google's rich results and the knowledge panel (structured data):**
- Machine-readable data describing the practice, all four physicians, every service,
  your hours, your real 5.0 rating and review count, and individual patient reviews.
  This is what feeds the stars, the map, and the business card Google shows.

**To protect and grow reputation:**
- Your real reviews are surfaced on-site and linked back to Google and Zocdoc, with the
  ratings marked up so search engines trust and display them.

**To convert the traffic once it arrives:**
- A trust bar ("no upselling, insurance checked in advance, second opinions welcome") on
  every service page, the care-finder funnel, and a lead-capture system that collects
  only non-medical contact details, so it is private by design and ready to feed a
  follow-up workflow.

**To stay fast, accessible, and safe:**
- The site is built to load fast on a phone (which is where most local searches happen),
  meets modern accessibility standards without a bolt-on "accessibility widget" (which
  reduces legal exposure for a medical practice), and collects no protected health
  information in any web form.

**Bonus, for the Fort Lee financing story:**
- A private operations and "growth snapshot" dashboard (demo data for now) that shows how
  patient demand can be tracked by service line and location, exactly the kind of
  reporting a lender or partner wants to see before backing a second office.

  ongoing.
