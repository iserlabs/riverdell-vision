# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Families across Bergen County, New Jersey searching for an eye doctor: parents
booking a child's first exam, adults with a medical concern (dry eye, diabetic
screening, sudden floaters), and patients other practices refer out for
specialty care (myopia management, Ortho-K, scleral lenses, vision therapy,
neuro-optometric rehabilitation). Many arrive from Google or Zocdoc on a phone,
comparing Riverdell against retail chains and other local optometrists. A
bilingual English/Korean audience is explicitly served. Their job: decide, in a
few minutes, that this is a serious, physician-led practice worth trusting with
their family's eyes, then book.

## Product Purpose

The public marketing site for Riverdell Vision, a physician-led family optometry
practice in Oradell, NJ preparing to finance a second office in Fort Lee. The
site exists to convert searchers into appointment requests (Zocdoc booking,
request form, phone) while establishing medical authority and warmth. It doubles
as a pitch prototype: alongside the marketing pages sits a zero-PHI operations /
investor dashboard on sample data. Success = booked appointments and a credible,
premium impression that reads "real medicine, not a glasses shop."

## Brand Personality

Physician-led, warm, and unhurried. Three words: **calm, credentialed, candid.**
The voice is a careful clinician who explains rather than upsells: medicine
first, honest options, never a push to buy frames. Copy is plain and specific,
never salesy. The emotional goal is earned trust and relief, the feeling of
having found a practice that takes eye health seriously and will keep your family
for years. Proof is real and verifiable (genuine 5.0 across 448 Google reviews,
4.9 on Zocdoc, verbatim patient quotes, named credentialed doctors).

## Anti-references

- **Retail optical chains** (LensCrafters/America's Best): eyewear-first,
  conveyor-belt, discount-driven. The site explicitly contrasts against this.
- **Generic AI medical/SaaS template**: Inter + cards-in-a-grid + gradient
  hero-metric blocks + stock "doctor with tablet" imagery. Reads as untrusted.
- **Over-clinical hospital coldness**: sterile blue corporate healthcare with no
  warmth or personality.
- **Manufactured hype**: invented testimonials, inflated numbers, aggregateRating
  schema the practice can't substantiate. Honesty is a hard constraint; only real,
  attributable proof ships.

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what you preach.** The site should feel as careful and unhurried as
   the exam it describes: generous spacing, considered typography, nothing rushed
   or shouty.
2. **Show real proof, never manufacture it.** Verbatim reviews, named doctors,
   real numbers, the actual office. If it can't be substantiated, it doesn't ship.
3. **Physician-led, not retail-led.** Medicine and expertise lead; eyewear and
   selling are downstream. Hierarchy and copy reinforce this at every turn.
4. **Editorial calm over decoration.** An indexed, ruled, "no-box" editorial
   system (serif display + grotesk body + mono labels) carries the premium feel;
   effects earn their place. Motion enhances an already-visible page.
5. **Warm, not cold.** Boutique warmth (the real space, a bilingual team families
   keep for decades) balances the medical rigor.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text meets >=4.5:1 on its surface (palette tuned so
`teal` and `clay` clear AA as text on the light `bone` surfaces; dark sections
use `brass`/`bone`, never `clay`, for labels). 44px minimum touch targets on
mobile, visible focus indicators, semantic headings and landmarks, and full
`prefers-reduced-motion` fallbacks (scroll reveals and count-ups degrade to the
static, already-visible value). Bilingual English/Korean audience considered in
copy. Automated axe + no-JS + touch-target checks run as Playwright e2e gates.
